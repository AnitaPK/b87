Krishna Janmashtami celebrates the birth of Lord Krishna, the eighth avatar of Lord Vishnu. Observed on the eighth day (Ashtami) of the dark fortnight in the Hindu month of Bhadrapada, it involves midnight vigils, fasting, devotional singing (bhajans), and re-enacting playful stories of Krishna's childhood.
